Hi, I'm Katelyn

I'm a cognitive science student at UCLA who builds technology with one question in mind: How do humans actually learn, think, and grow—and how can AI support that instead of short-circuit it?

Most people building AI for education are either engineers who don't understand learning, or educators who don't understand AI. I'm bridging that gap.

My background in cognitive science taught me how memory works, how attention develops, how young minds process information differently than adult ones. My work in civic tech—like building an interactive map that helped protect 250+ million acres of public lands—taught me how to translate complex systems into tools people can actually use.

Now I'm combining both: using what we know about human cognition to build AI that actually serves how young people learn.

I'm especially interested in:

  • AI in education that respects developmental psychology, not just engagement metrics
  • Algorithmic accountability for platforms that shape young minds
  • Youth-centered policy around AI governance and digital rights
  • Personalization without pigeonholing - how AI can adapt to learners without limiting them
